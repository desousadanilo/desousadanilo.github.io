Agent Based Systems

In recent years, the proliferation of agent-based systems has significantly transformed various sectors, including business, healthcare, and logistics. Agent-based systems (ABS) refer to computational systems where autonomous agents, often modelled after real-world entities, interact within an environment to achieve specific objectives (Oliveira, 1999). This discussion explores the factors contributing to the rise of these systems and examines the benefits they offer to organizations.

According to Leitão (2009), Organizations increasingly require scalable and flexible solutions to adapt to rapidly changing market conditions and customer needs, specially in the manufacturing industry to respond to high made of bespoke products. ABS offer modularity, decentralization, autonomy, scalability and re-usability of manufacturing control systems allowing the addition or removal of agents without disrupting the overall system. Their flexibility is evident in the ability to modify agent behaviours and interactions, making them ideal for evolving organizational contexts.

ABS allows organizations to simulate various scenarios and assess potential outcomes, leading to more informed and strategic decision-making (Julian & Botti, 2019). For instance, an agent-based model utilized empirical data from various cities to simulate the developmental trajectory of eco-protection and high-quality development under different policy scenarios. This model illustrates how economic development is influenced by ecological and resource constraints, and how policy interventions can shape developmental trends (Mathieu et al, 2023).

The rise of agent-based systems is driven by advancements in Artificial Intelligence and computing, the need to manage complex systems, interdisciplinary research, and the demand for scalable and flexible solutions. These systems offer numerous benefits to organizations, including enhanced decision-making, improved efficiency, better customer experience, robust risk management, and a platform for innovation. As organizations continue to navigate an increasingly complex and dynamic landscape, the adoption of Agent-based systems will likely play a crucial role in driving success and sustainability.
