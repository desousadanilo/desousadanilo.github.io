The 4th Industrial Revolution


The impact of industry 4.0 on Agriculture

The term "Industry 4.0", also known as Fourth Industrial Revolution, comprises a variety of concepts , and technologies like Internet of Things, big data analytics, cloud computing, cyber-physical systems (CPSs) and blockchain technologies (Devezas at el, 2017; Xu at el, 2021). The convergence of Industry 4.0 and agriculture opens the door to revolutionising conventional farming into the upcoming era known as Agriculture 4.0 (Liu at el, 2021).

Agriculture 4.0 offers a range of solutions aimed at tackling various challenges in agricultural food production, encompassing issues related to farm productivity, environmental consequences, food security, crop losses, and overall sustainability (Maffezzoli at el, 2022). This work is a brief discussion about the impact of the Fourth Industrial Revolution in Agriculture.

According to Araújo at el (2021), the use of data is not new in Agriculture. In addition to that, a study carried out by Scuderi at el (2022) shows that the advent of Industry 4.0 technologies such as Internet of Things, sensors, mobile devices, smart tractors and drones for precision farming, and cloud computing, big data, artificial intelligence for smart farming formed the model for Agriculture 4.0. Smart agricultural practices are utilised to improve the accuracy of applying fertilisers, pesticides, and herbicides. Drones are employed for weed detection, while robots assist in tasks like milking animals and removing weeds, benefiting farmers ( Javaid at el,2022).

Agriculture 4.0 introduces its own set of challenges, given that the technologies of Industry 4.0 bring both opportunities and challenges. The excessive collection and sharing of data might raise concerns about privacy and data security, as well as the potential for misuse of sensitive information (Da Silveira at el, 2021). Additionally, the high upfront costs of adopting advanced technologies could create barriers for small-scale farmers, exacerbating inequality within the agricultural sector (Sinha & Dhanalakshmi, 2022). Furthermore, the rapid pace of technological advancements could potentially lead to job displacement in certain agricultural sectors (Singh & , Yogi, 2022), impacting rural communities that rely heavily on agricultural employment.

In summary, the convergence of Industry 4.0 and agriculture is driving a remarkable transformation, promoting greater productivity and sustainability, while requiring careful consideration of the associated challenges to ensure a balanced and equitable agricultural future.
